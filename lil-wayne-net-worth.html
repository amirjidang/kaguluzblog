Lil Wayne's Unyielding Commitment To Rap, From The Beginning

Dwayne Michael Carter Jr. came into the world on September 27th, 1982, in impoverished circumstances.

Jacida Carter, Lil Wayne's then 19-year-old mother, and his father, Dwayne Carter, lived in a poverty-stricken area in the Hollygrove neighborhood of New Orleans, famously known as the 17th Ward.

When Lil Wayne was just two years old, his parents separated, and his father permanently abandoned the family. At 21 years old, Jacida, with her two-year-old son Dwayne, was trying to survive in the world.

The circumstances were harsh, but Jacida didn't back down. She is the kind of mother who kept her son Dwayne close, no matter the circumstances.

She fiercely fought to make a way financially while mired in poverty, working to provide for her son.

After several years of struggle, Jacida's circumstances eased when she married Reginald "Rabbit" McDonald. Lil Wayne was so attached to McDonald that he considered the man to be his "real father."

Lil Wayne, at the age of eight, started to compose raps, but when he reached the age of 12, he became eager to start his career in earnest. Wayne's mother advised against it.

However, this future rap icon was so determined (and dismayed at the prospect of another path) that he gravely injured himself in a youthful, misguided attempt to prove to his mother that he was unwilling to live without his future rap career.

There was speculation that Wayne didn't intend to attempt to take his own life or that it was an accident, as his mother reluctantly later mused:

I still don't know today. Was he playing with the gun or was it an accident?

I be wanting to ask him but I never asked him after all these years ... I never really found out about what really happened with him and that shooting.

However, Lil Wayne has addressed the question several times, and stated that it was not an accident.

Like in Solange's track "Mad," Wayne confessed: "And when I attempted suicide, I didn't die… I remember how mad I was on that day… Man, you gotta let it go before it get up in the way."

Lil Wayne also crafted verses to recall the experience in his song "Let It All Work Out," writing about the incident as well as his near-death experience (NDE):

"I aimed where my heart was pounding… I shot it, and I woke up with blood all around me… It's mine, I didn't die, but as I was dying… God came to my side and we talked about it."

After this incident, Wayne's mother relented, and the talented 12-year-old tween started penning musical verses, astonishing everyone with his immense and unique talent.

Lil Wayne's Early Career, And Unexpected, Tragic Loss

At the young age of 12, Dwayne started his musical career; this path was not easy, but he was confident that he would power through any roadblocks or setbacks, exclusively through his talent.

But some challenges are beyond a person's expectation.

While Lil Wayne was engrossed in launching his career in 1996, he learned that the man he considered his real father (McDonald) had been murdered.

Lil Wayne had come up against something he'd never anticipated, because the person who had supported him in pursuing his musical career was suddenly taken from him and his mother, leaving them to fend for themselves once again.

As he accepted an award in February 2023, 27 years after McDonald's murder, Wayne remained visibly distressed when discussing his father's sudden death:

But by this time, Lil Wayne had a purpose in his life, and he started to hustle to create his musical career – during the same acceptance speech, he recalled the juxtaposition of his rise to fame and his family's bereavement:

"Where I’m from? I walked into my mama’s room when I was 14, she asked me for a kid because my dad was killed. And her son had just blew up and went on his first tour. And we didn’t know that b*** was gonna be six months. When I came home she said, 'Son, I can’t live in this house by myself. We gonna have to figure something out.'"

Just months after his father’s death, Carter joined the hip-hop group Hot Boys, with rappers Juvenile, B.G., and Turk.

Hot Boys found instant local success when their first album, Get It How U Live by Cash Money Records, which sold over 300,000 copies in New Orleans. The album reached at number 37 on the Billboard Top R&B Albums chart in 1997.

After an impressive first album, Hot Boys released Guerilla Warfare in 1999, the turning point of every Hot Boys rapper’s life.

The album peaked on the Billboard Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums chart, and rocketed to the number five position on the Billboard 200. The album sold nearly two million copies, and was certified Platinum by the RIAA.

A 16-year-old Lil Wayne had often faced difficult times, but soon started to reap rewards for his sacrifices, hard work, and impeccable talent.

Lil Wayne’s Journey To Stardom

Lil Wayne gained fame through Hot Boys, but his solo debut album, Tha Block Is Hot, became even more significant in terms of sales than the Hot Boys' breakout album, Guerilla Warfare.

Five-time Grammy winner Lil Wayne's first album sold 229,000 copies in its first week.

This marked a decisive moment in his life, opening a path to becoming one of the most influential rappers in the world.

His road to becoming a rap legend was not easy; after his humongous outright success, many anticipated Lil Wayne would conquer the rap arena without any further hurdles.

But Lil Wayne’s second album Lights Out only sold 116,000 copies in its first week and barely managed to sell a million copies, lower than his debut album's sales.

His third album, 500 Degreez, endangered Lil Wayne’s musical career, selling only 103,000 copies in its first week, topping out at around half a million copies sold.

It seemed like Wayne's initially promising career was starting to stall. Critics and rap fans claimed that his music was one-dimensional, centered around cars and women.

Lil Wayne was facing one more problem: his group Hot Boys had broken up, and every Hot Boys rapper other than him left Cash Money Records. The Grammy winner became frustrated with record label politics.

He postponed his fourth album, took some time for introspection in terms of his career, moved from New Orleans to Miami where he sought new creative partners, and in two years, a new Lil Wayne emerged – ready to become one of the greatest rappers.

Lil Wayne’s Musical Legacy

Dwayne Michael Carter Jr., known as Lil Wayne, has sold more than 200 million records, and is considered among the world's highest-selling musical artists.

The American Music Award winner has sold 25 million albums and 92 million digital tracks in the United States.

In 2012, Lil Wayne achieved the incredible feat of surpassing the legend Elvis Presley with the most entries (109) on the Billboard Hot 100. He was the first to break "The King's" record, and did so again in 2020.

Now, 186 of his songs have reached the Billboard Hot 100 chart.

By the time 2010 rolled around, Lil Wayne was among the most influential musicians in the game.

What We Know About Lil Wayne's Net Worth

Lil Wayne's net worth has widely been reported as standing at between $150 and $170 million, a sum currently repeated across the internet as fact, and to exclusion.

Consequently, nearly every estimate of Lil Wayne's net worth has been placed in that range for several years.

In March 2023, remarks made by Lil Wayne himself with respect to his net worth in 2016 began recirculating.

Wayne explained that he was aware his net worth was estimated at $150 million at the time, and he vehemently contested the figure:

In response to the financial speculation, Wayne said:

"Honestly, I'm gonna let everybody know now, when you go check a m**********r's net worth and that s*** be saying some crazy numbers, I don't have a cent close to that s***... I don't have that, but I guess they be meaning that's what I'm worth."

He continued, joking about firing his agent, due to the disparity between Google results and Lil Wayne's net worth in actuality:

"So I be always calling my agent and being like, 'I'm telling you, bro, I'm gonna fire the s*** out of you, I Googled that, you know what I mean? I'm like, I'm a zillion-dollar rapper on Google, bro. It's motivation, that's all. It's just motivation."
