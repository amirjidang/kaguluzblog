While more men experience hair loss or balding, women may have more ‘hair cares’ and worry about hair loss. Female and male hair loss patterns have some differences, but many forms are considered hereditary.

Male Pattern Hair Loss

  • The most common male hair loss types are receding hairline or balding on the top of the head.
  • It affects 30-50% of men by the age of 50.

Female Pattern Hair Loss

  • This pattern is often characterized by a noticeable thinning occurs or a widening of a specific balding area, usually on the top of the head.
  • Female hair loss usually happens after menopause as a result of hormone changes.

FAQs:

Why does hair start thinning?

A common reason for hair loss is the aging process. Hair tends to change as we mature and go through hormonal changes. It often thins, becomes more brittle, and grows more slowly. Some people notice a gradual loss of hair, while others believe that sudden hair loss is associated with hormonal changes.

Environmental factors can also play a critical role in increasing hair loss. Pollutants, toxins, and poor diet can contribute to hair loss. Stress is also another critical factor in hair restoration. Some people with high-stress levels report shedding or clumps of hair that fall out easily during challenging times.

Illness can contribute to hair loss—patients with severe COVID-19 claim that they noticed hair loss after contracting and recovering from the virus.

Do hair growth supplements work?

Many customers claim that hair growth supplements work. They reported better overall hair health and regrowth in areas that were bald or thinning. They may also report less shedding and better hair density.

Several research studies also indicate that supplements have a positive effect on hair regrowth and health. One study showed that zinc, pumpkin seed oil, and other supplements may have beneficial effects on hair regrowth.

How long does it take to see results?

It is common for customers to notice improvements within 3-6 months. Improvement depends on the type and severity of symptoms. Consistent use is critical for achieving the desired results.

If you stop taking hair loss supplements, will you lose hair again?

Hair loss might continue after you stop treatment. A significant factor is the cause and severity of hair loss, but it is important for anyone using hair growth supplements to use products consistently. Whether hair loss is minimal, moderate, or extreme, it is important to stay consistent with treatment for optimal results.

Can hair growth supplements regrow hair in bald spots?

It is possible to regenerate hair in bald spots. Although many factors influence this process, many customers report a positive change in hair health and specifically bald spots. Hair regrowth depends on health conditions, consistent hair growth supplement use, stress, and environmental factors.

Is it better to take a supplement or use a product designed for external application?

Both forms have their advantages, but many people report fewer negative side effects with topical hair growth products.

Some people prefer tablets and capsules over topical products because they find it inconvenient to apply and rub in the topical treatment. Others may find taking vitamins and tablets unpleasant in general, so they might prefer a product for external use.
